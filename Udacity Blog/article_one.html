Digital Project Manager Salaries: What You Need to Know

The demand for digital project managers is growing as more companies invest in creating websites, mobile apps, and other online platforms. The Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts that employment of web developers will grow 13 percent from 2016 to 2026. This growth is much faster than the average for all occupations. If you’re considering entering this lucrative career or curious about what a Digital Project Manager does, keep reading!

What does a Digital Project Manager Do
A digital project manager is responsible for overseeing and executing digital projects, usually within a company. Those who have extensive experience managing large-scale digital projects can command even higher salaries. This will involve working with a team of developers, designers, and other professionals to ensure that the project is completed on time and within budget. In addition, you will be responsible for ensuring that the project meets all of the requirements set forth by the client.

How Experience Impacts a Digital Project Manager’s Salary
Digital Project Managers are in high demand as more and more businesses move their operations online. With the right skills and experience, a Digital Project Manager can expect to earn a good salary. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a Digital Project Manager is $85,486 per year. However, salaries can range from $64,000 to $154,000 per year depending on experience, location, and company size. 0-1 year of experience: $84,292 4-6 years of experience: $96,714 15+ years of experience: $127,012 Digital Project Managers with several years of experience can expect to earn on the higher end of the salary range. In general, the more experience and expertise a Digital Project Manager has, the higher their potential earnings will be.
